Working with image groups

If more than one image is specified for the taxon in the knowledge-base they can be displayed as a special image group where, you can change the image by clicking on another camera icon at the top of the image. The camera icons become visible - one for each available image - when you move your mouse cursor over the top of the image. Alternatively use the left and right arrows, visible when you move your mouse cursor to the left or right of the image, to move forward and backward through the image group. You can close an image by clicking the cross in the top right of it.

You can zoom in and out of the image by using your mouse wheel. When an image is zoomed in, you can pan it by dragging the image with your mouse.

If your image group is shown in a separate window, you can make it bigger or smaller by dragging the bottom right corner of the window. Move the window around the screen by dragging with the mouse. (If the window is zoomed in, dragging the mouse pans the image rather than dragging the window in which case you should drag the window's caption bar.) If you have a lot of image windows open and you want to close them all at once, then right-click the mouse to see a context menu and select the Close all images item.
